Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

During my group project that I had to complete last year, we divided the tasks into parts and shared it among the group members. We had a month to complete the project and set a weekly meeting. The meetings were ineffective as some members did not complete some promised tasks and had no clear picture of the final product of the project. It felt as if we required more regular meetings and break down of tasks into smaller parts to bring more flexibility within our project. Some were just reluctant to contribute towards the project and this definitely hurt the productivity of the project. The strategy that I would use would be based on an agile environment where we would have regular meetings (sprint) and low fidelity and/or high fidelity prototype would be created earlier in the project.
